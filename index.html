Science and Heritage Research Initiative (SHRI)

A Gallery for Scientific Sanskrit Texts in Indian Heritage with Multi-modal Features

Key Words: Scientific literature in Sanskrit, AI-based Sanskrit OCR, Audio-embedded text, Information retrieval System for Sanskrit, Technical dictionary in Sanskrit, Interactive web-portal

About The Project

The continuous quest and insatiable thirst that Indians had for knowledge resulted in the creation of a huge corpus of both tangible and intangible heritage. While the intangible heritage in the form of yoga and other spiritual practices that got developed over ages in India is fairly known, and recorded in the annals of history, the scientific literature in diverse disciplines such as astronomy, mathematics, medicine, logic, arts, linguistics and so on have remained neglected and scantily studied.

The Cell for Indian Sciences and Technology in Sanskrit (CISTS) at IIT Bombay established in 2002, primarily serves as a unique center dedicated to promote high-quality research in the vast scientific literature in Sanskrit. Through this proposed project we aim at creating a relevant technological ecosystem that aids the process of collection, preservation and digitization of texts and manuscripts pertaining to ancient scientific literature and their dissemination in various forms.

We aim to create a digital space for the Scientific Heritage of India by providing the most significant texts as a digitally searchable, audio-embedded hypertext document gallery along with a Scientific Sanskrit lexicon bank for researchers and students of the premier universities and academic institutions of India to benefit.

Key Objectives of the project

Create a collection of digital versions of the most significant sanskrit texts in various scientific domains starting from astronomy and mathematics, with multi-modal features which include...

  • Automatic character recognition based text from scanned images.
  • Highly reliable texts as a digital version that are thoroughly verified by domain experts .
  • Ability to index and search within the scanned texts.
  • Audio-embedded source text to enable both listening to the chant and a mode that enables learning to recite.
  • Texts hyperlinked and supplemented with digital dictionaries containing domain-specific technical terms in Sanskrit.

Development and application of the necessary technologies that are required to build the following...

  • AI-based OCR technologies to scan the images of texts, books and manuscript copies in Sanskrit and turn them into searchable text.
  • Information retrieval system to index and search texts in Sanskrit.
  • Creation of Sanskrit lexicon bank for the scientific texts.
  • An interactive web platform to host the gallery of books along with audio-embedded digital version and deployment of a Sanskrit digital dictionary for domain-specific keywords.
